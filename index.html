About Me

There are many things that I value most in my life, such as culture and education. Yet it is technology and computer science which has fueled my overarching ambitions to pursue computing as a future career.

Love - Computing

I first fell in love with computing when I drew a version of the cartoon Mickey Mouse with Visual Basic in middle school. Since then, I have dedicated myself to acquiring relevant education and work experience to grow my skills and knowledge in this field.

Passion - Education

My other passion lies in education. I enjoy the feeling of helping others since I was in primary school, which is why I volunteered to teach Cantonese every week during high school. Now I became a curriculum developer in Girl Coders, an organization which aims to attract more women to be interested in STEM-related fields.

Root - Culture

I believe my international education in China, Canada, and the US has broadened my global perspective and strengthened the respect I have for my own culture. Furthermore, embracing other cultures has enriched my life a lot.

Lifetime Goal

These experiences in the spheres of education, culture, and working with other communities abroad has brought me great joy and satisfaction whilst applying technology to several projects has allowed me to enrich young people’s lives.

My father always says that values come from giving back to the community – that is a lifetime goal for me.
